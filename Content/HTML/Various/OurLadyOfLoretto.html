Our Lady of Loretto Church Building in Longbottom


The old Our Lady of Loretto church building in Longbottom was dedicated in 1872.  It was the mission church until 1972 when the church was moved to Tuppers Plains near the fire station.  Our Lady of Loretto mission was closed in the early 90s and now Sacred Heart parish covers all of Meigs County. The church building in Tuppers Plains was sold years ago, but the church building and cemetery in Longbottom is still part of Sacred Heart parish.

Since the church building in Longbottom is no longer a mission church the Diocese will not let us celebrate the sacraments there.  The Diocese has also said that we are not allowed to use parish money to fix up the building. But, private donations can be used.

The old church building is in a state of disrepair and is slowly falling apart.  It is shameful to let a former house of God remain in this shape.  It needs to be either fixed up or torn down.

A meeting was held on July 7th to discuss the future of the building.  Another meeting will be held on Sunday, September 1st, at 11:00am.  Requests for quotes of both tearing down the building and fixing it up have been obtained. 

No matter what happens to the old church building the cemetery will remain an active cemetery.
